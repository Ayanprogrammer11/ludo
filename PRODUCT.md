# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Friends, families, and casual players who want a familiar Ludo match across a shared screen or a private online room. Online players are signed in so rooms, reconnection, and match history can be tied to real accounts.

## Product Purpose

Ludo provides a polished, server-authoritative version of the classic board game with local preview play, private multiplayer rooms, reconnection, account-backed results, and replayable match history. Success means the game feels fair, resilient to real-world interruptions, and easy to return to after a session ends.

## Brand Personality

Warm, trustworthy, and quietly playful. The interface should feel like a cared-for tabletop game rather than a casino, generic dashboard, or loud arcade skin.

## Anti-references

Avoid overdecorated game chrome, casino styling, dark sci-fi dashboards, marketing-heavy screens that delay play, and UI that hides important match state behind novelty. Do not make controls or timers feel punitive; they should communicate fairness and flow.

## Design Principles

- Put the current match state first: whose turn, how long remains, who is connected, and what action is available.
- Preserve trust through clear server-authoritative feedback, account-scoped history, and predictable recovery from disconnects.
- Keep the board readable: color should help identify players and legal actions without overwhelming the tabletop.
- Make real-world interruptions graceful: reconnecting, skipped turns, and completed games should be explicit and understandable.
- Let history teach the match: past games should replay as a timed sequence rather than a static receipt.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Use visible focus states, readable contrast, non-color text labels for status, and reduced-motion alternatives for animated turn indicators and timers. Preserve keyboard access for buttons and replay controls.
